To formulate the algorithm, I've treated the battery system as a linear programming problem. The following are the objective function, parameters, decision variables and operational constraint for the linear programming model. The objective function is the objective we would like to maximise, which is the revenue over the time horizon of optimisation.

  • The objective function:
    $\sum_{t=1}^{T}{P_t}(\frac{D_t{E}}{4}{\eta}-\frac{C_t}{4{\eta}})$

  • Parameters:
    $T$: Time horizon of arbitrage
    $P_t$: Spot price at time t
    $E$: Charge and discharge efficiency of the battery, I took 0.9.
    $\eta$: Marginal Loss Factor, I took 1 in this case for simplicity.

  • Decision Variables:
    $Capacity_t$: Opening Capacity at time t
    $C_t$: Charge Power at time t
    $D_t$: Discharge Power at time t

  • Opertaion Constraint:
    $Capacity_t = {Capacity_{t-1}} + \frac{C_t}{4}E - \frac{D_t}{4}$
    $0 \leq {Capacity_t} \leq 1 (or 2), \forall t \in T$
    $0 \leq {C_t} \leq 1, \forall t \in T$
    $0 \leq {D_t} \leq 1, \forall t \in T$
    $D_t \leq \frac{Capacity_t\times{E}}{4}$
    $when\; P_t < 0,\:D_t = 0$

You could see from the upper formular, I built an optimizer based on 15 mins time interval, which works both for our 15 mins and 60 mins data.
